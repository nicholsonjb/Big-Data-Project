This study will use logistics regression to predict if an infant has a low birth weight.

Low birth weight, defined as weight less than 2500 grams, is an outcome that has been a concern to medical physicians for years. Infant mortality rates and congenital disability rates are very high for low birth weight babies. A womans behavior (such as diet, smoking habits, and receiving prenatal care) during pregnancy can significantly alter the chance of carrying the baby to term, and consequently, of delivering a baby of healthy birth weight. The low birth weight data includes infant ID number, birth weight, smoker or non-smoker, mothers age, race, history of premature labor, history of hypertension, presence of uterine irritability, and the number of physicians visited during the first trimester. The goal of this study is to determine whether these factors could be critical predictors of low birth weight.

Low Birth Rate Data¶

Analyze the random sample of low birth rate data.

Dataset source Hosmer, D.W., Lemeshow, S. and Sturdivant, R.X. (2013) Applied Logistic Regression, 3rd ed., New York: Wiley

The low birth weight dataset is also part of the aplore3 R package.

Variable Description
0 ID Identification Code.
1 LOW Low birth weight 0 = greater than or equal 2500g, 1 = less than 2500g
2 Age Mothers age in years
3 RACE Race (1: White, 2: Black, 3: Other)
4 SMOKE Smoking status during pregnancy (1: No, 2: Yes)
5 PTL History of premature labor (1: None, 2: One, 3: Two, etc.)
6 HT History of hypertension (1: No, 2: Yes)
7 UI Presence of Uterine irritability (1: No, 2: Yes)
8 FTV Number of physician visits during the first trimester (1: None, 2: One, 3: Two, etc.)

LabeledDocument = Row("ID", "PersonInfo", "label")

#Function that parses each raw in CSV file and returns an object of type LabeledDocument

def parseDocument(line):
    values = [str(x) for x in line.split(',')] 
    if (values[1]>'0'):
     Low = 1.0
    else:
      Low = 0.0
     
     #PersonInfo is the contancation of Age, Race, Smoke, History  of premature labor, History of hypertension,Presence of Uterine irritability, and Number of physician visits during the first trimester 
    textValue = str(values[2]) + " " + str(values[3])+" " + str(values[4] + " " + str(values[5]) + " " + str(values[6]) + " " + str(values[7]) + " " + str(values[8]))
    return LabeledDocument(values[0], textValue, Low)
